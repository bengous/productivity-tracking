# 🧠 The Psychology of Work

**Goal:** Work *with* your brain's natural tendencies, not against them.
*Reference: [The Psychology of Work](https://super-productivity.com/guides/the-psychology-of-work/)*

## 1. Dopamine: The Seeking Chemical

*   **Myth:** It's a "pleasure" chemical.
*   **Reality:** It's a "**seeking**" chemical. It drives you to look for rewards.
*   **The Hack:**
    *   **Procrastination:** Your brain is seeking the *easy* dopamine of social media over the *hard* dopamine of finishing a task.
    *   **The Fix:** Make the reward visible. Every time you check off a task in Super Productivity, you get a small, visual dopamine hit, which trains your brain to seek the "hard" reward.

## 2. Energy, Not Time, is the Limit

*   **Myth:** You have 8 hours in a day.
*   **Reality:** You have maybe **4 hours of real, focused energy**.
*   **The Hack:**
    *   **Ultradian Rhythms:** Your brain works in 90-120 minute cycles. Pushing past this leads to burnout.
    *   **The Fix:** Use Time Boxing and take **real breaks** (step away from the screen). A "break" is not checking email.

## 3. The Zeigarnik Effect: The Open Loop

*   **Effect:** Your brain is obsessed with **unfinished tasks**. This is why you feel anxious when you have 50 things on your mind.
*   **The Hack:**
    *   **Externalize:** Get every single task out of your head and into Super Productivity. This tells your brain "it's captured, you can relax."
    *   **The Fix:** The "Brain Dump" at the start of Time Boxing is not just organization, it's a mental health exercise.

## 4. Task Initiation Friction

*   **Problem:** The hardest part of a task is starting.
*   **The Hack:**
    *   **Implementation Intentions:** Create a clear trigger. "When I finish my coffee, I will open `api.ts`."
    *   **The Fix:** Your `sync.sh` script is an Implementation Intention. "When I sit down to work, I run `sync.sh load`."
